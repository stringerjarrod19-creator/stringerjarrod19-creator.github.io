Feature Writing 2024

V360 Award Winner Leads Breakthrough in Migraine Drug Discovery

Accenture · V360 Award Feature · Global internal audience

Did you know that we are using gen AI to turn computers into science labs?

The pharmaceutical industry is being transformed by AI. Technologies that promise a quicker, cheaper path to clinical trials are replacing traditional methods.

Team Lundbeck (EMEA), V360 Award winner for Co-innovation, used the power of gen AI and computer simulations to help develop new migraine medication. Our team partnered with Lundbeck, a global pharmaceutical company and client, to identify medications with AI. By taking this approach, what would've taken 5 years to go to clinical trials took 18 months. Costs that would have exceeded $650 million totaled $2 million instead.

"This discovery process is time consuming and costly, and we wanted to find a more efficient way to aid in identification of new drug targets."

— Morana Vitezic, Lundbeck scientist and partner

How they did this

Conducting scientific experiments on computers, not in labs, made these advances possible. Team Lundbeck discovered connections between genes and headaches entirely by digital means. They found new ways to alleviate migraines by using technology to better understand how they happen.

Behind Team Lundbeck's success and V360 Award are a talented group of people. Medb Corcoran, Luca Costabello, Christophe Gueret and Meabh Redmond are part of The Dock, our global R&D and innovation center in Dublin. They were joined by Ashni Sedani, out of London, and Mette Steffensen, the Client Account Lead out of Denmark. With the impressive work they did on this project, they raised the bar for others in the industry.

"We used Accenture's tried and tested innovation methodology to be able to win and deliver."

— Medb Corcoran, Accenture lead for Team Lundbeck

Implications for the future

Team Lundbeck's achievements highlight the potential of AI in drug development and tackling complex health challenges. Their approach is not confined to a single industry — it can be applied across industries around the world, from agriculture to packaging to skincare.

They are already applying it in the beauty space. As they did with Lundbeck, they are harnessing the power of gen AI to help diamond client L'Oréal develop new ways to treat skin conditions. As this V360 Award-winning team is showing, the future is bright and full of possibility.
